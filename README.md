## 📚 Portfolio Contents & Project Breakdown

### Part 1: DC Circuit Analysis & Operating Points (Labs 1–4)
* **Lab 1: Two-Loop DC Circuit Validation** – Simulating a multi-loop DC environment to verify handwritten operating points and branch currents.
* **Lab 2: Nodal Analysis** – Implementing nodal networks in Multisim and confirming voltage values with MATLAB math and graphs
* **Lab 3: Two-Mesh KCL Validation** – Proving Kirchhoff's Current Laws (KCL) mathematically on paper and creating simulations to prove voltage across nodes.
* **Lab 4: Three-Mesh Network** – Tracking simultaneous voltage drops and branch currents across multiple resistors (R1–R5) using virtual DC operating point tools.

### Part 2: Transient Responses & Time Constraints (Labs 5–6 & 8)
* **Lab 5: Transient Graphing & Waveforms** – Visualizing capacitor charging sequences using three distinct styles of transient output waveforms.
* **Lab 6: Dual-Switch Capacitor Charging & Discharging** – Used two separate switches to control a capacitor: one path forces it to charge up slowly (50kΩ path), while the second path forces it to empty out in a fast, sudden burst of electrical current (20kΩ path).
* **Lab 8: Inductor Energy Storage & Decay** – Analyzing transient voltage steps across resistors and observing inductor energy decay behaviors under strict timing constraints.

### Part 3: AC Steady-State & Filtering Applications (Labs 7, 9–11)
* **Lab 7 & 9: Sinusoidal Steady-State AC Networks** – Modeling parallel circuit branches under sinusoidal excitation to observe time-varying phase shifts between supply voltages and total branch currents.
* **Lab 10: RLC Circuit Resonance & Admittance** – Mathematical and visual breakdown of a complex RLC topology, calculating admittance, magnitude, and precise phase angles.
* **Lab 11: Filter Engineering (Notch & Low-Pass)**
  * *Part 1:* Constructing a 1.59kHz Notch Filter to isolate and attenuate undesirable frequencies using an AC Sweep.
  * *Part 2:* Designing an RLC Series Low-Pass Filter mapped across a decade-scale decibel scale (100 points/decade) to visualize frequency roll-off.
